About Me

My name is Alp Altug, and I am a Software Development Engineer at Amazon, bringing a robust technical and mathematical background to the forefront of innovation. My academic journey culminated in an Honors Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science from the University of California, Berkeley (Class of 2024). This strong educational foundation has been the bedrock of my career, enabling me to tackle complex challenges with a blend of analytical rigor and creative problem-solving.

My professional ethos is centered on a relentless pursuit of excellence and a passion for developing impactful technological solutions. I have consistently demonstrated expertise in diverse areas such as Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, cloud infrastructure, and full-stack web development. My experiences, ranging from pioneering research at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory and Nokia Bell Labs to driving product development in dynamic startup environments like 11Sight and shuffl mobility Inc., have solidified my ability to deliver scalable, user-centric applications. At Lawrence Livermore, I delved into deep learning research for anomaly detection in particle physics, while at Bell Labs, I explored blockchain technologies by building a decentralized marketplace. As a developer at 11Sight, I engineered multimodal AI agents, and as a co-founder and CTO at shuffl, I led the development of a ride-hailing mobile application from concept to MVP.

I am driven by a desire to innovate and collaborate, always focusing on how technology can be harnessed to achieve significant organizational goals and create value. My work as a quantitative researcher, both at the Wheeler Water Institute focusing on environmental sustainability and at Prime Trading in the financial sector, further underscores my versatility and my commitment to applying data-driven insights to solve real-world problems. I thrive in environments that demand continuous learning and adaptation, and I am always eager to explore new frontiers in technology. You can reach me at alpaltug@berkeley.edu or alpaltug@amazon.com.
